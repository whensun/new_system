The mseccfg CSR holds configuration bits that control access to the entropy‑source seed CSR (sseed and useed), enable the Zicfilp extension via the MLPE bit, and modify PMP behaviour through the RLB, MMWP and MML fields (other bits are reserved). The mseccfg CSR configures the Smepmp security extensions and contains the fields MML, RLB, MMWP, MLPE and the two‑bit PMM field (bits 33‑32). When MML=1, PMP entries are interpreted according to the truth‑tables of rules 53‑69: entries with L=1 are enforced only in Machine mode, entries with L=0 only in Supervisor/User mode, and each combination of R,W,X bits yields the access permissions or access exceptions described in those rules; MML becomes locked when set, requires MML=1 to use the Shared‑Region RW=01 encoding, and together with RLB controls whether shared‑region executable entries may be created. The RLB field governs the immutability of locked PMP entries: if RLB=0, any entry with L=1 cannot be cleared or modified and writes to RLB are ignored while such an entry exists; if RLB=1, the immutability restriction is lifted, locked entries must have higher priority, shared‑region executable entries are allowed, and writing RLB=0 locks the field, after which the boot process must clear and lock RLB, and any programmed locked entry makes all currently cleared bits of mseccfg permanent. If MMWP=1, the field locks and Machine mode is prohibited from reading or executing any address not explicitly permitted by a PMP entry; if MMWP=0, Machine mode may access unmapped addresses, and the field becomes immutable after being set. Writes that violate a field’s WARL constraints or target a locked field are ignored; reads of MLPE provide the architectural xLPE predicate regardless of supervisor‑mode presence, and bits 33‑32 reflect and can enable pointer‑masking for Machine mode.
